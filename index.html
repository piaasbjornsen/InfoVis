Unmasking terrorism

Beyond the headlines

Terrorism is a war where the front lines are within the depths of our minds. Media outlets flood our screens and front pages with alarming stories, but how deadly is terrorism truly?

First we need to understand what we mean by terrorism

Many of us have strong associations to the word 'terrorism', evoking thoughts of violence, tragedy, innocence lost, anger, and the complexity of politics and religion. But what exactly is it, terrorism....

Let's look at some definitions

European Union

According to EU law, terrorist offences are acts committed with the aim of: seriously intimidating a population unduly compelling a government or international organisation to perform or abstain from performing any act seriously destabilising or destroying the fundamental political, constitutional, economic or social structures of a country or an international organisation

The Netherlands

Terrorism involves crimes committed for ideological motives. Terrorists focus on preparing or committing serious acts of violence. Typically, these acts aim to disrupt society with the goal of bringing about societal changes, instilling serious fear in the population, or influencing political decision-making. Terrorism and online hate speech manifest in various forms on the internet. Radical and terrorist expressions can be made online, including texts and videos that call for acts of violence.

United Nations

Terrorism is the use of violence against civilians to instill fear with the goal of political influence. Terrorism is an illegal method of violence and a form of violent extremism.

The common thread in these definitions of terrorism is the use of violence or the threat of violence for ideological purposes. Whether it's intimidating a population, compelling a government or organization, or destabilizing fundamental structures, terrorism involves actions aimed at creating fear, influencing political decisions, and causing significant societal disruption.

Now, let us have a look at how widespread it is, and has been throughout the years

In the below graph you can see the number of casualties, which is the combination of deaths and injuries occured by a terrorist attack for the last 30 years. We can see that there is an overall increase in the average number of casualties from 1970 to 2020. This can potentially be explained by two main reasons. First, advancements in technology and especially advancements in military technologies may have played an important role in amplifying the overall toll of casualties during this multi-decade period. Secondly, the escalating geopolitical conflicts and increased intensity of armed conflicts in several parts of the world have collectivelly contributed to this rise.
